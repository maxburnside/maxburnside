  • 2022 Apple
    2022 Apple MacBook Air 13-inch
    • M2 (8-core CPU, 10-core GPU)
    • 24GB
    • 1TB

    The first MacBook Air I owned was a 2012 model. It was lightweight, had great battery life, and enough power for most tasks. Since then, the only Apple laptops I used were MacBook Pro models. In getting my latest machine, however, I decided to opt for a MacBook Air. I love the size of the machine and prefer it to even 14-inch MacBook Pro models for portability. Battery life is the best I've experienced in a laptop, and the power is nearly always capable enough. There are some tasks that push the processing to its limits, but I'll take the trade-off for all of the other upsides. Absolutely would recommend this computer.

    This is a backup computer in the event that something happens to the M2 MacBook Air. While performance is acceptable most of the time, it reaches unusably hot temperatures too often and has a relatively short battery life - making it a machine I don't recommend. Luckily, the most recent generation of MacBook Air and MacBook Pro (with M1 and M1 Pro/M1 Max chips, respectively) models are much better in the heat and battery life category. Having used Apple laptops for over 20 years (dating back to a 333MHz PowerBook G3 Lombard), this is the first one that has disappointed me.

    Originally purchased with the intention of being an air-gapped, cold storage wallet for cryptocurrency, I now use this Chromebook for responsive UI and performance tests. Plus, the 2-in-1 tablet functionality makes it a nice, dual-purpose device. The R11 is an older model now, but there are updates from Acer that look to have even more features, such as the Spin 3.
